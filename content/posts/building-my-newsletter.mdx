This site now has a newsletter and a blog. Both are deliberately small: plain
MDX files in the repo, a single email field, and no dashboard.

I write a post as an `.mdx` file, save it locally, and publish it with one
command that commits and pushes. Subscribers get a short email when something
new goes up. That is the whole system.

<Note>This box is a React component imported straight into the post — proof that MDX can pull in anything from the codebase.</Note>

## Why local-first

Keeping posts as files means writing feels like editing code: version
controlled, greppable, and editable from anywhere. No database to back up, no
CMS to log into.

If you want the occasional note when I publish, the [newsletter](/#newsletter)
is right on the homepage.
